Minnesota

I was born and raised in Eagan, Minnesota. I attended school in Eagan from Kindergarten to 8th grade, after which I moved to Eden Prairie. This was a scary move, because I had to start high school without knowing anybody! However, it all worked out for the best. I made the Varsity Gymnastics team, became the Vice President of Competitive Events for DECA, participated in Model UN, and founded the Helping Hands club where we sewed mittens for homeless shelters in the winter. After high school, it was time to head off on a new adventure to college in Madison, Wisconsin.

Wisconsin

I attended college at the University of Wisconsin Madison. My major was Engineering Mechanics and Astronautics and my minors were Computer Science and Mathematics. I joined the Robotics team my first year of college, where I had my first introduction to C. I was also on the NAIGC Gymnastics team for the first two years of college, where I went on to compete at Nationals and qualify for the Balance Beam finals. I also became the STEM Liaison for the Campus Women's Center my second year, and hosted several workshops about Imposter Syndrom for women in STEM. I started working for Maydm as a teacher my third year, which was an organization dedicated to bringing young women of color into Computer Science. Finally, I started working at the Computational Nuclear Engineering Research Group my third year, which is where I mastered Python, Git, and Docker. I am so thankful for my experiences in college, and I wouldn't trade them for the world.

Hungary

I decided to study abroad in Budapest, Hungary at the Budapest University of Technology and Engineering, which is the oldest engineering university in Europe! I took classes such as Algorithms, Vibrations, and Information Security. While I was there, I had the opportunity to travel to France, Sweden, Austria, Slovakia, the Czech Republic, Ireland, Spain, Poland, Croatia, Germany, Belgium, Italy, and Vatican City with my friends! It was amazing to learn about the rich history in all of these places, but I am so glad I chose Budapest as my home base. It will always have a special place in my heart.

Virginia

The summer before my last year of college, I managed to land a Software Engineering internship with General Dynamics Mission Systems. I had so much fun that I decided I wanted to work for this company when I graduated from college. I applied for something called the Engineering Leadership Program, where young engineers could do three rotations, each one year in length, at a selection of GDMS facilities around the country. I had always wanted to live on the East Coast, so my boyfriend and I packed up and headed out to Arlington, Virginia where I had secured a position as a Software Developer on the Tactical Control System program, which is the software that is on the Navy's submarines, for my first rotation. We had so much fun living so close to Washington D.C. and experiencing the beautiful parks, delicious food, and world-class museums.

North Carolina

After my rotation on TCS was done, it was time to move on to my next rotation in Greensboro, North Carolina. I am currently working as a Software Developer and the Post Mission Analysis Lead of the Knifefish program, which is an unmanned underwater vehicle that hunts for mines in a combat area. I have only been here for a short time, but I am learning so much and I can't wait to see what the next seven months have in store for me.
